What is Kadaster (Cadastre)?

The Dutch Land Registry that records all real property, ownership, and encumbrances.

Key Facts — Kadaster (Cadastre)
  • Official government agency maintaining the Dutch Land Registry
  • Records ownership, boundaries, and encumbrances
  • Information is publicly accessible online and through requests
  • Maps digitized and regularly updated with property changes
  • Essential for validity of property transfers and mortgages

The Dutch Kadaster is the official government agency responsible for maintaining the Land Registry (Landregister) that documents all real property in the Netherlands. It records ownership, boundaries, mortgages, easements, and other property-related information. The Kadaster is an essential institution for Dutch property law and real estate transactions.

The Kadaster maintains detailed cadastral maps and registers showing the precise location and boundaries of each property parcel. These maps have been digitized and are accessible online, making property information readily available to interested parties. The system provides transparency and facilitates real estate transactions.

Property information recorded by the Kadaster is public and can be accessed by anyone through online searches or by requesting certified documents. This accessibility helps prevent fraud and facilitates due diligence in property transactions. Buyers and lenders can verify ownership and identify encumbrances.

Registration with the Kadaster is essential for ownership and mortgage rights to be fully valid and enforceable. Transfers of property and mortgages must be officially registered to take effect. The registration system is linked with notarial and deed-filing systems to ensure comprehensive documentation.

The Kadaster also maintains the BRK (Basisregistraties Kadaster), a comprehensive database of all property-related information. This system allows for quick searches and provides detailed property information. The digital infrastructure has significantly improved the efficiency of property transactions in the Netherlands.

Common Mistake: Relying on informal records or oral agreements instead of Kadaster registrations. The Kadaster is the legal authority for property information in the Netherlands.
Expert Tip: Search the Kadaster website before making an offer on property. This quick search shows ownership, mortgages, and other encumbrances, giving you valuable information for negotiation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I search the Kadaster for property information?

The Kadaster website allows free searches by address or parcel number. You can view basic information online and request certified extracts.

What information appears in a Kadaster extract?

The extract shows owner identification, property description, any mortgages, and other registered encumbrances.

How quickly are transactions recorded with the Kadaster?

Once a notarial deed is executed, registration typically occurs within a few weeks.
