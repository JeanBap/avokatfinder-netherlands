Property & Tax

What is WOZ-waarde (Property Valuation)?

The official government valuation of Dutch property, set annually by municipalities and used to calculate property tax (OZB), wealth tax (box 3), and mortgage assessments.

Key Facts — WOZ-waarde (Property Valuation)
  • Official municipal property valuation determined annually (January 1)
  • Used to calculate municipal property tax (OZB), wealth tax (box 3), and mortgage assessments
  • Differs from purchase price, mortgage appraisal, and cadastral value
  • Reassessed every 6 years with updates in between
  • Owners receive annual WOZ-beschikking; can be challenged within 6–8 weeks

The WOZ-waarde — stands for "Waardering Onroerende Zaken" (Valuation of Immovable Property) — is the official estimated market value of residential and commercial real estate in the Netherlands as determined by each municipality's WOZ assessment office. Every January 1st, each municipality reassesses all properties in its jurisdiction and assigns a WOZ-waarde, which is used as the basis for calculating municipal property tax (OZB — Onroerende Zaak Belasting). The WOZ-waarde is different from the actual purchase price, the appraised value for mortgage purposes, or the cadastral value (WEK) used in historical land records.

The WOZ-waarde is determined by the municipality's assessment office using a valuation model that considers the property's size, condition, location, recent comparable sales in the area, and other physical characteristics. The valuation is typically conducted every six years, with updates in between years to reflect market changes. Property owners receive an annual WOZ-waarde notification (WOZ-beschikking) by January 1st, showing the estimated value used for tax purposes. This notification can be challenged if you believe the valuation is incorrect.

The WOZ-waarde directly affects your annual property tax bill. The municipality calculates OZB by applying a percentage rate (which varies by municipality, typically 0.1–0.3% of WOZ-waarde) to your property's assessed value. A higher WOZ-waarde means a higher tax bill. In addition, the WOZ-waarde is used to calculate wealth tax (box 3 inkomstenbelasting) for property owners, and it influences mortgage lender decisions during refinancing. Understanding your property's WOZ-waarde is important for budgeting and assessing whether the assessment is fair.

You can challenge your WOZ-waarde if you believe it is inaccurate. Most municipalities accept written objections (bezwaar) if filed within 6–8 weeks of receiving your WOZ-beschikking. To support a challenge, gather evidence such as comparable property sales in your neighborhood (within the last 2 years), professional appraisals, photographs of property defects, or details of planned renovations. Many property owners hire an independent appraiser to build their case. Successful challenges can result in a significant reduction in your property tax bill.

The WOZ database is also used for other purposes beyond property tax. Banks and mortgage lenders reference it during lending decisions. Insurance companies use it to set property insurance premiums. The WOZ-waarde is publicly available — you can look up any property's valuation on the municipality website or through the national WOZ database. For expat property buyers, the WOZ-waarde becomes relevant in the year following purchase; your first tax bill will reflect the municipality's assessed value, not necessarily what you paid.

Common Mistake: New property owners assume their annual property tax will be based on the purchase price they paid. In fact, the municipality sets the WOZ-waarde independently; it may be higher or lower than your purchase price. Budget for property tax based on the municipality's estimated value, not the price you paid.
Expert Tip: If you purchase a property below market value (e.g. at a foreclosure or through a private sale at a discount), your WOZ-waarde may be significantly higher than your purchase price. In the year following purchase, you will receive a WOZ-beschikking; if it seems inflated, file a bezwaar with comparables. An independent valuation may strengthen your case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I appeal my WOZ-waarde?

Yes. You have typically 6–8 weeks from the date of your WOZ-beschikking to file a written bezwaar (objection) with the municipality. Provide comparable sales data, professional appraisals, or photographs of property defects to support your case. Many successful appeals result in reassessments.

How does WOZ-waarde affect my mortgage?

Mortgage lenders use the WOZ-waarde as one reference point during lending decisions and refinancing. A lower WOZ-waarde can help your case during refinancing. Lenders also conduct their own appraisals, so the final loan amount depends on multiple factors.

Is the WOZ-waarde available to the public?

Yes. The WOZ-waarde for any property in the Netherlands is publicly searchable on most municipality websites and through the national WOZ data portal. You can look up any address to see the estimated government valuation.
